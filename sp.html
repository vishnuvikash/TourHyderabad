Sanjeevaiah Park

Located by the side of Hussain Sagar Lake, Sanjeevaiah Park enjoys a prime location, at the centre of the city. Named after the former president of India, Dr. Neelam Sanjeeva Reddy, this park is maintained by the Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority. Initially there were plans to connect Sanjeevaiah Park to Lumbini Park, which is located on the other side of the lake through an aerial tramway. However, later the proposal wasn’t approved. A popular tourist spot, this park also has the fame of receiving the Best Open Landscape Award in 2010, under the category of Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage. It is also known to be one of the largest and the oldest parks in Hyderabad.

Sanjeevaiah Park is sure to captivate you with its natural beauty. Its lush greenery and it’s refreshing environment are sure to rejuvenate you. The view of the Hussain Sagar Lake from here allures all its visitors. People can come and relax amid the refreshing quietness of the park.

Along with the scenic beauty, it also boasts of rich fauna. This park has nearly 100 species of avian fauna, comprising of both resident and non-resident birds. During the winter season, you can see many more species of migratory birds from all over the world. There are about 50 species of insects and butterflies in this park. One of the commonly spotted birds in this park is the Pied Crested Cuckoo, that is popularly considered an indication for the arrival of monsoon rains. Although, many of the other common avian visitors such as cormorants, jacanas, spot bills, common coot, cranes and purple herons are not seen in the park since 2010, when the construction work started in the park.

Sanjeevaiah Park which is just beside Necklace Road is perfect for a relaxed picnic with family and friends. Sit and enjoy the sprawling greenery around you, away from the traffic and noises of the city streets. The most popular feature of this park is the wide array of roses here. Its rock garden and floral clock also captivate everyone. It is an ideal place for families to for a day outing. There is ample of area for children to play as well. Adults can also play games like Frisbee, etc.

A leisure walk amid the greenery is also a common activity here. A separate pavement has been made for the joggers, and for those who would like to stroll around the park. The park is also candidly called as the Lover’s Park, for the number of couples who come here to sit and spend some time together amid its beautiful surroundings. It is also a common venue for many events, ranging from roller skating races to social awareness campaigns.

Open on all day of the week, you can visit Sanjeevaiah Park. Its timings are from 8.00 AM to 7.00 PM. The entry fee at the Sanjeevaiah Park is also minimal. If you are a regular visitor, you can also opt for a monthly or yearly pass.
